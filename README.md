# Trader Behavior vs Market Sentiment Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the relationship between **cryptocurrency market sentiment** and **trader behavior/performance** using historical trading data from Hyperliquid and the Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index.

The goal is to uncover patterns in trader performance during different market sentiment conditions such as **Fear, Greed, Extreme Fear, Extreme Greed, and Neutral**, and derive insights that can help improve trading strategies and risk management.

---

## Objective

The main objectives of this project are:

- Analyze how **market sentiment affects trader profitability**
- Identify patterns in **trader behavior under different sentiment conditions**
- Examine **risk-taking behavior such as trade size and position direction**
- Generate insights that could help design **smarter trading strategies**

---

## Datasets Used

### 1. Hyperliquid Historical Trader Dataset

This dataset contains detailed trading activity from the Hyperliquid trading platform.

Key columns include:

- **Account** – Trader account identifier  
- **Coin** – Cryptocurrency traded  
- **Execution Price** – Price at which the trade was executed  
- **Size Tokens** – Trade size in tokens  
- **Size USD** – Trade size in USD  
- **Side** – Trade direction (Buy / Sell)  
- **Timestamp** – Time of trade execution  
- **Closed PnL** – Profit or loss generated by the trade  
- **Fee** – Transaction fee  

---

### 2. Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index

This dataset represents overall cryptocurrency market sentiment.

Columns include:

- **timestamp**
- **value**
- **classification**
- **date**

Sentiment categories include:

- Extreme Fear  
- Fear  
- Neutral  
- Greed  
- Extreme Greed  

---

## Methodology

The analysis follows a structured data science workflow.

### 1. Data Cleaning
- Convert timestamp columns into datetime format
- Standardize date formats across datasets
- Handle missing values

### 2. Data Integration
- Extract trade dates from the trader dataset
- Merge trader data with the Fear & Greed dataset using the **date** column

### 3. Feature Engineering
- Create a **win/loss indicator**
- Analyze trading performance metrics
- Examine trade size and position behavior

### 4. Exploratory Data Analysis

The following analyses were performed:

- Profit distribution across sentiment categories
- Win rate comparison by sentiment
- Trade size behavior across sentiment conditions
- Long vs short trading patterns
- Top trader profitability analysis

### 5. Visualization

Visualizations were created using **Matplotlib** and **Seaborn** to identify patterns and trends between market sentiment and trader performance.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Higher Risk-Taking During Greed Markets

Traders tend to take **larger trade positions during Greed and Extreme Greed periods**, suggesting increased confidence and higher risk appetite.

### 2. Profit Distribution Varies with Market Sentiment

The distribution of profits and losses changes across sentiment categories, indicating that **market psychology influences trading outcomes**.

### 3. Aggressive Trading During Extreme Sentiment

Extreme sentiment periods often correspond with **larger trades and higher volatility**, indicating aggressive market participation.

### 4. Potential Contrarian Opportunities

Fear-driven markets may present **contrarian trading opportunities**, where panic selling leads to oversold market conditions that can later reverse.

---

## Trading Strategy Insight

A sentiment-aware trading strategy could integrate the Fear & Greed Index as a **market risk indicator**.

For example:

- **Extreme Fear** → Possible buying opportunities due to panic-driven selling  
- **Extreme Greed** → Increased caution due to market overconfidence  

Using sentiment indicators can help improve **risk management and timing decisions**.

---

- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
